Operating Principles

Doctrine

GhostLedger is infrastructure. These principles define what it does — and what it deliberately does not.

Three boundaries

Detect ≠ Accuse
GhostLedger identifies patterns in reported claims. It does not accuse, name, or condemn any entity. Detection is signal analysis, not judgment.
Coordinate ≠ Enforce
GhostLedger organizes information and connects claimants with qualified professionals. It does not file legal actions, issue demands, or enforce outcomes.
Route ≠ Represent
GhostLedger routes claims to professionals who may choose to assist. It does not provide legal advice, legal representation, or act on behalf of any party.

Operating principles

01
Claimant-initiated only. Every claim enters because a person filed it. GhostLedger does not scrape, surveil, or generate claims from external sources.
02
Evidence over narrative. Claims advance based on material evidence — not volume, emotion, or public pressure. The system measures documentation, not outrage.
03
Human oversight at every step. No claim is escalated, no professional is contacted, no action is taken without human review. Automation handles scoring and routing. Humans make decisions.
04
Transparency without exposure. Claimants can check their status at any time. Internal scoring logic, thresholds, and respondent intelligence are not publicly disclosed.
05
No fees collected. GhostLedger does not charge claimants. Professionals in the Independent Legal Fellowship set their own terms independently.
06
Coordination, not activism. GhostLedger exists to make recovery possible — not to campaign, advocate, or take sides. The system is neutral infrastructure.

Scope of Operations

GhostLedger coordinates recovery efforts for unpaid wages, withheld funds, and platform disputes. It does not provide legal advice, legal representation, or financial advice. Nothing on this site constitutes a legal opinion or determination of liability.
